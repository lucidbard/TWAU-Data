\begin{abstract}
  PianoFlow is a demonstration of the potential for augmented reality
  games to increase player engagement through rich, timely feedback,
  show how a control scheme can effectively repurpose existing input
  devices through gaze detection and alignment. It draws from previous
  piano-tutor applications, Synesthesia and  designs both in the visualization of the control
  commands. This paper discusses the design process, the game itself,
  and the lessons learned as well as a planned evaluation.
